Week 08
20 FebPre-war posture
IranMiddle East

US strikes against Iran assessed as likely within weeks

Several dozen US aircraft and air defence systems redeployed into theatre. Indirect Geneva talks on 17 February yielded no narrowing of positions. A sustained US operation lasting several days assessed as plausible.

USANorth America

Partial US government shutdown begins, 14 February

Airport security checkpoint workers unpaid. No major disruption yet, but unplanned absences expected to compound after several weeks based on past shutdowns.

ItalyEurope

24-hour transport sector strike called for 26 February

Major union plus airline, airport and ground staff unions striking concurrently. A comparable strike in September 2025 produced 70+ cancellations across Bologna, Florence, Milan and Naples.

SomaliaEast Africa

Al-Shabaab attack on Mogadishu airport possible ahead of May–June elections

Group struck the airport and adjacent military facility five times in 2025. Most likely method: mortar strike.

NepalSouth Asia

Wildfire season opens 1 March — visibility impacts expected through May

Crop burning combined with winter smog. In 2025 poor visibility closed Pokhara airport for several days.

Week 09
27 FebD-1
IranMiddle East

Strike imminent: second carrier in eastern Mediterranean

US authorises departure of non-essential staff from Israel on 27 February. Hezbollah signals it would intervene militarily if Khamenei is targeted; Lebanese FM warns Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport "might" be struck in retaliation.

Pakistan/AFGSouth Asia

Pakistan strikes Kabul, Kandahar, Paktia on 27 February

Retaliation for Afghan offensive. Civilian planes continue overflying Afghanistan above 30,000ft and operating out of Kabul, per online flight trackers.

MexicoLatin America

Jalisco cartel violence suspends Guadalajara, Puerto Vallarta flights, 22–23 Feb

Authorities seized "rocket launchers capable of shooting down aircraft" from CJNG. No evidence of intent against civil aircraft; capability assessed as present at low altitudes.

MyanmarSoutheast Asia

Drone strikes flight on the ground at Myitkyina, 20 February

Anti-junta drone reportedly downed by authorities and hit the plane. Co-located civil-military airfields highlighted as exposure points; no signs anti-junta groups intend to target civilian aircraft.

Week 10
06 MarWar opens
Middle EastGulf · Levant

Hundreds of drones and missiles fired by Iran since 28 Feb

Targeting US bases, interests and energy infrastructure across Arab Gulf states. Coastal airspace largely closed; Israel strikes southern Beirut, southern Lebanon and Bekaa Valley.

UAEGulf

Emirates and Etihad announce limited resumption

Emirates plans return to 100% of network in coming days. Flight paths avoid coastal areas where Iranian strikes are concentrated and skirt military sites.

LebanonLevant

Beirut Airport operational but evacuation routes constricted

Israeli strikes ordered in Dahiyeh, where Highway 51 (the main airport route) runs. MEA appears to be the only carrier flying; many flights sold out.

GlobalWorldwide

Strait of Hormuz effectively blockaded — fuel disruption looms

Iranian blockade disrupts shipments of jet fuel and crude. Europe assessed as most exposed to prolonged shortage.

AzerbaijanCaucasus

Drones strike Nakhchivan International Airport, 5 March

Four drones entered Azerbaijani airspace; two struck the airport and an adjacent school. Footage suggests Iranian make. Iran denied responsibility.

HaitiCaribbean

US ban on landings in Port-au-Prince extended to 3 September

Ban first imposed 2024 after three US carriers struck by gunfire. Zed Airlines suspended Port-au-Prince operations in late January.

Week 11
13 MarAttrition
Middle EastGulf

Iran capable of sustaining strikes for at least a month

Drone and missile launches against Saudi Arabia surge from 7 (10 March) to 58 (12 March). Riyadh airspace closures expected during overnight defence firings.

UAEGulf

Four debris-impact incidents around Abu Dhabi and Dubai airports since 7 March

Risk highest at Abu Dhabi due to co-location with an Emirati air base. No evidence Iran targets airports directly; debris from interceptions of nearby Iranian projectiles.

USANorth America

Hours-long delays at Charlotte, Houston, New Orleans

Compound effect of unpaid security workers; lawmakers remain split on immigration agency reforms tied to the shutdown.

GlobalAir cargo

Air cargo capacity down 22% in first week of war

Limited operations at Doha and Dubai dragging the global figure. Jet fuel prices have doubled since 28 February. South Asia–Europe routes hit hardest.

Week 12
20 MarSpillover
UAEGulf

Drone hits Dubai airport fuel tank, 16 March

Fourth drone or debris incident near a major UAE airport since the war began. Iran appears to be targeting US military and radar facilities adjacent to civilian airports.

Pakistan/AFGSouth Asia

Afghan drones strike Pakistani cities, 13 March; Islamabad airspace briefly closed

Tit-for-tat strikes accelerating. UK aviation authorities issue NOTAM warning of heightened military activity in Kabul.

EuropeContinent-wide

Kerosene prices up nearly 90% since February

Some carriers have raised fares or cancelled flights. Most European carriers say current-year supply is adequate; widespread cancellations not expected this month.

KenyaEast Africa

Heavy rains divert Nairobi-bound Kenya Airways flights to Mombasa, 7 March

Rainy season delays expected to extend through May, possibly into June this year per local met agencies.

Week 13
27 MarCargo squeeze
QatarGulf

Iran halts attacks on Qatar; Qatar Airways increases frequencies, 27 March

No recorded Iranian attacks on Qatar since 20 March. Doha hosts US forces and remains a plausible future target.

AsiaRegion-wide

Vietnam, Philippines warn of route cuts and possible groundings

Carriers cutting short, low-margin routes first. Longer routes including Europe-bound at risk if Hormuz disruption persists for months.

Sub-Saharan AfricaContinent-wide

70% of regional jet fuel supply transits Strait of Hormuz

Kenya and South Africa hubs hold roughly a month of reserves. Extended disruption would force route cuts.

USANorth America

ICE deployed to airports, 23 March; LaGuardia fire-truck collision, 22 March

Two deaths from "series of failures" at LaGuardia. FAA flags 150 airports with "increased risk" of runway incursions.

RussiaEastern Europe

Ukrainian drone strikes close airports in Kazan, Moscow, Saint Petersburg

Three or more strikes on Baltic-coast oil and gas terminals in past week. Telegram posts suggest Ukrainian drones may have crossed Polish and Baltic airspace.

Week 14
02 AprLate escalation
USANorth America

Trump orders back pay for security workers, 30 March

Staff largely returned, wait times improving. Continued funding uncertain; Congress recessed.

UAEGulf

39 flights at Dubai within minutes of incoming launches since 28 Feb

Per Wall Street Journal, 21 March. Dubai briefly suspends operations 30 March after debris from interception lands nearby.

LebanonLevant

Hezbollah claims SAM launch at Israeli jet over Beirut, 27 March

Group has downed several Israeli military drones with SAMs since 2023. Continued launches heighten misidentification risk for civilian aircraft.

UKEurope

Last scheduled Gulf jet fuel cargo to UK arriving — 31 March

UK historically imports 55% of kerosene from the Gulf (OPIS). Average of eight cargoes from the Gulf in transit at any one time. Several weeks needed to fully reroute supply chains.

Nordics/BalticsNorthern Europe

Three Ukrainian drones crash in Estonia and Finland

Likely caused by Russian electronic warfare. Finnish jets scrambled to drones, 29 March. Misidentification risk over Baltic and Finnish airspace assessed as elevated.

Week 15
10 AprFragile pause
Middle EastGulf

Ceasefire takes effect 7–8 April; Iraq, Bahrain reopen airspace

Reports of multiple Iranian attacks on Gulf states since the ceasefire. WSJ: at least five commercial planes parked in the Gulf damaged by falling shrapnel since 28 February.

AsiaRegion-wide

Acute jet-fuel shortages in Philippines, Pakistan

Countries with limited refining capacity hit hardest. US and Iran continue to differ on how Hormuz shipping should resume.

ChinaEast Asia

Unusually long airspace reservation over Yellow Sea/East China Sea until 6 May

No altitude limit; satellite imagery suggests military exercises. Beijing launched landing drills on Nantian island, Zhejiang, 4 April. Fourth such large reservation in recent years.

KoreaEast Asia

North Korea conducts three missile launches over Sea of Japan, 7–8 April

Pyongyang signalled intent at Ninth Party Congress to develop weapons systems complementing nuclear capability. Past tests have broken up or veered off course.

Week 16
17 AprFuel crunch
KuwaitGulf

Kuwaiti airspace remains closed; airport heavily damaged

Closed since 28 February. Iranian drones reportedly damaged terminal, radar, and fuel tanks at the country's only civilian airport, per state media.

UAE/QatarGulf

Emirates and Etihad at ~70% pre-war ops; Qatar Airways above 50%

EASA still advises against operations in UAE and Qatari airspace. Non-Gulf carriers expected to take longer to return.

EuropeContinent-wide

IEA chief: Europe has "maybe six weeks" of jet fuel — 16 April

Several major airlines cut short-haul flights. UK, Netherlands, Germany flagged as most exposed due to Gulf import dependency.

AsiaRegion-wide

Laos, Myanmar, Pakistan, Philippines, Vietnam exposed to fuel-driven cancellations

Limited refining, high import dependence on Gulf or neighbours, low FX reserves. Asian neighbours have halted jet-fuel exports.

SchengenEurope

EES biometric system live since 10 April — three-hour border delays reported

Per Airports Council International Europe. Airlines reportedly leaving without delayed passengers rather than holding flights.

NigeriaWest Africa

Leaked memo: jihadists planning attacks near Nnamdi Azikiwe International, Abuja

Government memo dated 13 April flags "credible reports" of planning. Local press: security protocols strengthened from 16 April. Memo not independently verified.

ColombiaLatin America

Heavy fog disrupts El Dorado, Bogota, 15 April

Several hours of restricted operations and dozens of countrywide cancellations. Typical for the April–May and September–November rainy seasons.

AfricaSub-Saharan

Carriers in talks with government after threats to suspend operations. Kenya and South Africa carriers also warning of disruption. Small regional carriers most exposed.

Several regional airlines cancelling flights. Multilateral crisis pact on 16 April unlikely to resolve shortages while Hormuz traffic remains constrained.

Up 5.1% the week before. Driven by elevated jet fuel and looming supply gaps. Prices unlikely to ease while Hormuz traffic remains near-standstill.

USANorth America

Near-miss between two planes near Nashville, 18 April

ATC directed one aircraft into another's path. Per AviatorDB: such incidents up 61% from 410 (2019) to 660 (2025). FAA hiring exceeds targets but attrition and long training cycles delay relief.

RussiaEastern Europe

Ukrainian drones close ~25 Russian airports for hours since last week

Some targets more than 1,000 km from the Ukrainian border. Per Russia's military, Kyiv launched more drones than Moscow in March — the first time since the war began.

Five threads that defined ten weeks.

No single event explains the period. These intersecting pressures, taken together, do.

01 / Five
The 28 February inflection
Two weeks of public escalation signalling — carrier deployments, departures of non-essential US personnel, Hezbollah threats — gave way to open conflict. Gulf airspace fragmented immediately. Reopening has been state-by-state, conditional, and reversible.
02 / Five
Hormuz translates into a global fuel story
From Week 10 disruption became the dominant operational risk. Jet fuel doubled by Week 11, up 270% in Nigeria by Week 17. Europe at six weeks of stocks; Pacific Islands declaring emergencies. The conflict's economic shadow now reaches further than its kinetic one.
03 / Five
Misidentification as the dominant aviation risk
No party is targeting civilian aircraft. But debris, interceptions, low-flying defensive systems, and electronic-warfare-induced drone drift across Hormuz, the Baltic, the Caucasus, and the Korean Peninsula make accidental engagement the through-line of operational concern.
04 / Five
Conflict didn't pause everything else
A US shutdown stressed checkpoints and ATC. EES rollout caused three-hour Schengen queues. Pakistan-Afghanistan strikes intensified. Ukrainian drones reached 1,000 km into Russia. The intelligence picture is the war plus everything that didn't stop for it.
05 / Five
Ceasefire ≠ normalisation
The 7–8 April ceasefire reopened some airspace but EASA's bulletin remains active. Kuwait stays closed. Gulf carriers operate at 50–70%. Hormuz traffic at ~5% of pre-war volume. The aviation system is now in a third state — neither war nor peace, with elevated baseline risk.
→ Outlook
Watch the next thirty days
Three indicators worth tracking: Hormuz tanker traffic recovery, EASA conflict bulletin renewals, and whether Iran resumes attacks on the Gulf during Pakistan-mediated talks. Any of the three reverting will cascade through fuel, cargo, and overflight risk simultaneously.
